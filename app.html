Hexadecimal

Hexadecimal turns letters and numbers into number pairs.

Example: "Hello" becomes "48 65 6C 6C 6F"

Hexadecimal outputs your entire text and is case sensitive(outputs text exactly how you type them).

Ascii

Ascii gives every character a number.

Example: "Hello" becomes "072 101 108 108 111"

Ascii outputs your entire text and is case sensitive(outputs text exactly how you type them).

Binary

Binary reprensents text and numbers as 0's and 1's.

Binary outputs your entire string and is case sensitive(outputs text exactly how you type them).

Base64

Base64 represents text and numbers as Ascii text format it is case sensitive(outputs text exactly how you type them).

Example: "Hello" becomes "SGVsbG8="

Ensure that when decoding Base64 the hash should end with an equal to ("=") sign or two for efficiency.

A1Z26

A1Z26 gives every letter it position number in the alphabetical order (it reprensents spaces(" ") as 0).

Example: "Hello" becomes "8 5 12 12 15"

AIZ26 outputs your entire string and is case insensitive(outputs all text to uppercase).

Morse

Morse uses dots and dashes to reprensent text and numbers.

Example: "Hello" becomes ".... . .-.. .-.. ---"

When using our morse translator ensure you do not add punctuations they could lead to errors and the "/" after morse codes signify a word space it is case insensitive(only ouptuts upper case text).

Reverse

Reverse is simply text obfuscation (types text backward and is case sensitive).

Example: "Hello" becomes "olleH"

Atbash

Atbash is a substitution cipher it substitutes each letter with it miror (A = Z, B = Y).

Example: "Hello" becomes "Svool"

Caesar

Caesar is a substitution cipher it shifts letters by a fixed number down the alphabets or up all depending on the operation set.
