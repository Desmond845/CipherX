Morse

Morse uses dots and dashes to reprensent text and numbers.

When using our morse translator ensure you do not add punctuations they could lead to errors and the "/" after morse codes signify a word space it is not case sensitive(only ouptuts upper case text).

Binary

Binary reprensents text and numbers as 0's and 1's.

Binary outputs your entire string and is case sensitive(outputs text exactly how you type them).

Base64

Base64 stores text and numbers as Ascii numbers it is case sensitive(outputs text exactly how you type them).

Ensure that when decoding Base64 the hash must end with an equal to ("=") sign or two for efficiency.

Reverse

Reverses the text (types them backward and is case sensitive)

Caesar Cipher

Caesar cipher shifts letters by a fixed number down the alphabets

Atbash Cipher

Atbash substitutes each letter with it miror (A = Z, B = Y)
